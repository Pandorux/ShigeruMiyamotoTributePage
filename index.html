Shigeru Miyamoto

The man that defined the game industry.

Shigeru Miyamoto is the creator of some of the most acclaimed and best-selling game franchises of all time, including Mario and The Legend of Zelda.

A timeline of Shigeru Miyamoto's Life

  • 1952 - Born in Kyoto, Japan
  • 1977 - Graduates from college with a Bachelor's Degree in Industrial Art & Design and thereafter was hired as a Concept Artist for Nintendo.
  • 1977 - Nintendo releases, Radar Scope, the first computer game that Miyamoto made a considerable contribution to;
  • 1980 - Nintendo releases, Radar Scope, the first computer game that Miyamoto made a considerable contribution to;
  • 1981 - After several years of Nintendo failing to break into the North American and on the verge of financial collapse, Miyamoto was tasked with converting unsold Radar Scope units into a new game. This game was the massive success Donkey Kong.
  • 1985 - Super Mario Bros. was released, a widely popular that pushed the sales of the Nintendo Famicom to found heights, directed, produced and designed by Miyamoto. Super Mario Bros. was so popular it managed to get into the homes of 40 million people worldwide.
  • 1986 - Miyamoto would release another outstanding and outstanding game, The Legend of Zelda. The Legend of Zelda would go on to be the first standalone game to sell over a million units in history.
  • 1988 - The third game of Super Mario Bros. 3, directed and heavily influenced by Miyamoto would go out to the world this year, becoming the best selling standalone game of all time.
  • 1993 - The release of Star Fox. The game was renowned for containing polygonal graphics that astonished gamers. Furthermore, Star Fox was so tremendously successful it allowed Nintendo to create a branch in Europe known as Nintendo of Europe.
  • 1996 - Release of Super Mario 64. A game upon release, the game sold millions upon millions of copies worldwide and to this day is considered one of, if not the most important video game title of all time.
  • 1998 - Miyamoto became the first person to be inducted into the Academy of Interactive Arts and Sciences' Hall of Fame.
  • 1998 - Miyamoto would go on to release what to this day is considered the greatest game of all time according to Metacritic and Gamerankings. The was called The Legend of Zelda and it revolutionized video games.
  • 2000 - To ensure that the GameCube would be successful on release, Nintendo had Miyamoto to create a new and unique title for the new console. Miyamoto first created a tech demo titled Mario 128 to showcase the unique features that the GameCube could performed where 128 marios would fall from the sky. This would later transform into the title Pikmin, where a Captain Olimar could command an army of 100 pikmin.
  • 2002 - Metroid Prime was released in 2002, the third game in the Metroid series and the first Metroid produced by Miyamoto. This was also the first first-person Metroid game and it ended up being the most highly praised game of the year.
  • 2003 - Another Zelda game called The Legend of Zelda: Four Swords Adventures for the gamecube was released in 2003, and was met with terrible sales. The game went on to become the worst selling game in the series, much to the dismay to Miyamoto, Aonuma and the developers behind the game. Throughout the year Miyamoto expressed his disappointment in the reaction to the game
  • 2004 - The Game Boy franchise's long reign was coming to an end. Nintendo had fully revealed its successor, the Nintendo DS. Nintendo made sure that people knew Miyamoto was working on Nintendo DS software, as this could've been the reason that the ill-fated Virtual Boy performed so badly - that Miyamoto didn't support it whatsoever.
  • 2006 - Miyamoto was placed at the helm of developing many of the triple-A titles for Nintendo's upcoming home family console, the Wii. The first two games Miyamoto would work on were Wii Sports and The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess. With Wii Sports being sold with the new console.
  • 2007 - Miyamoto would go on to create a the game concept of a new Mario game he had wanted to create showing the Mario 128 tech demo to the world in 2000 focusing on spheres and gravity. The game was Super Mario Galaxy and he stayed on-board to supervise the development of the game. When realised, Super Mario Galaxy ended up becoming the best rated game of the year and to date is the third highest rated video game of all time.
  • 2010 - Unlike in the 2000s in which he was involved on many projects as producer, Miyamoto's activities in development were less pronounced in that decade with Miyamoto only producing Luigi's Mansion: Dark Moon and Star Fox Zero in that decade. Otherwise, Miyamoto was credited as General Producer, Executive Producer and Supervisor for most projects, which are positions with much less involvement in comparison to a producer.
  • 2015 - Following Iwata's death in 2015, he and Genyo Takeda took up the positions of co-presidents in the interim period before the selection of a new president.
  • 2015 - On September 16, 2015, he left his managerial position as head of EAD and became a Creative Fellow for Nintendo. Since his promotion to Fellow, Miyamoto's involvement with game development has been reduced to a more supervisory role, as he has shifted to oversee some of Nintendo's newest venues, including the production of movies using Nintendo IP and the collaboration with Universal Studios for the development of the Super Nintendo World line of theme parks.
